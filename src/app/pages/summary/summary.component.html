Why did we start Project Arch?

With the pace of change and amount of digital disruption, the next horizon requires a new type of reimagining that focuses our workforce transformations toward a more customer-centric Organization. While some teams have planned efforts around workforce shifts, we’ve lacked a holistic execution plan that looks across Organization, streamlines investments, and accelerates the application of learnings. Simply put, Project Arch is aimed at asking ourselves the provocative questions and understanding the “art of the possible” for Organization.

We took several capability focus areas in the organization and did a deep-dive look at our current state, leveraged external benchmarking and put forth recommendations for how we might grow and evolve as an organization through three key levers: skills, location and automation.

The objective of creating a Organization Workforce Blueprint is to develop a future workforce strategy, bold actions, and multi-year plan to change the way we work by detailing:

  • the future skills we will need,
  • the best location where work can be done, 
  • and prioritizing digital and automation opportunities to achieve this objective
  • in order to achieve sustainable profitable growth

What’s next?

The next steps for Project Arch are about finding opportunities to operationalize what we’ve learned. We’ve compiled final recommendations across our capability focus areas and are working to integrate what we can as it relates to skills, location and automation opportunities into both our existing and upcoming work. We’ve shared our findings with leadership teams in our focus areas, and will partner with HR and regional Organization Development teams to determine how and what can be activated.

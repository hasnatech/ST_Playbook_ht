What does the TMO organization look like?

The TMO itself is a small centralized team that mobilizes and supports the transformation.

It sets and communicates program ambitions and intentions, tracks and monitors value generation, invests in, pilots and helps build new capabilities that will benefit all of Organization, and engages with all relevant stakeholders to coordinate and execute the decisions and priorities of the company.

Also embedded within the TMO are core capabilities that are key to our transformation, such as the new Data function, the ACI COE and Organization Business Services, and the TMO is closely partnered with the Commercial Excellence COE.

What are the Transformation Offices (TOâ€™s)?

To deliver on the set transformation ambition each part of the organization has setup their own team (transformation offices) which is lead by an Enterprise/Function Transformation Leader. The TMO provides oversight and connection to the network of transformation offices (TOs) which are working at the organizational nodes of the company (enterprise or function). The transformations are run and owned by these TOs which may also have sub-offices below them (i.e. at the group or sub-function level).

What does success look like? In What are key operating principles for the TMO to achieve success?

A strong TMO combined with the strong execution network through the TOs will help us change how we work so we free up time, talent and energy and deliver for customers. Bringing a strong focus on execution excellence and continuous improvement, the basic operating principles that support the delivery of our cost reduction, income generation and growth targets include:

  • Clear priorities with aggressive targets tied to business performance
  • Defined portfolio of initiatives that support the set ambitions, with established goals and timelines
  • Straightforward, achievable roadmap of sequenced projects
  • Structure, cadence and governance rigor that is right-sized and simplified to fit businesses and enable speedy decisions
  • Clear roles, responsibilities and capabilities to engage and empower employees to drive improvement
  • Accountability and ownership of value realization and capture
